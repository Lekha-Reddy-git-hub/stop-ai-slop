# Changelog

## v1.3.0 (2026-07-09)

Caught up with the current stop-slop and went past it.

- catalog.md: the fuller pattern list. Adds false agency (name the human),
  narrator-from-a-distance, passive voice, copula avoidance, negative listing,
  superficial -ing analysis, false ranges, aphorism formulas, elegant variation,
  diff-anchored writing, Wh- and "So"/"Look" openers, plus the significance,
  promotional, and vague-attribution vocabulary. All tiered, none blanket-banned.
- false-positives.md: what NOT to flag, and the signs of real human writing to
  protect. Clusters over isolated tells. This is the guardrail that keeps deslop
  from becoming a second slop, and it is what most removers lack.
- Credited Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" (via the humanizer skill, MIT)
  alongside stop-slop.

## v1.2.0 (2026-07-09)

The self-improving release.

- slop_miner.py: auto-discovers new tells using the excess-vocabulary method
  (Kobak et al. 2025).
- SELF-IMPROVING.md, CONTRIBUTING.md, and a slop-sighting issue template.

## v1.1.0 (2026-07-09)

- Renamed to stop-ai-slop.
- channels.md: per-platform slop for X, LinkedIn, cold email, warm email, long-form.

## v1.0.0 (2026-07-09)

First release, derived from stop-slop by Hardik Pandya (MIT).

- Tiered phrases, structures, formatting, sycophancy; before/wrong/right examples;
  slop_score.py; TESTS.md.
