# Before / wrong fix / right fix

The original stop-slop's examples broke its own rules: they traded slop for a
punchy, clipped second slop. Each example here shows THREE versions so the model
learns the difference between de-slopping and over-correcting.

---

## Example 1: Throat-clearing + emphasis crutch

**Before**
> Here's the thing: building products is hard. Not because the technology is
> complex. Because people are complex. Let that sink in.

**Wrong fix (over-corrected into punchy slop)**
> Building products is hard. Technology is manageable. People aren't.

*Why wrong: three clipped fragments and a binary contrast. That is its own AI
tell, not a fix.*

**Right fix**
> Building products is hard, and the technology is usually the easy part. The
> hard part is that people are complex, which no amount of good engineering
> solves.

*Removed the opener and the emphasis crutch; kept a normal, varied sentence.*

---

## Example 2: Filler + fake reassurance

**Before**
> It turns out that most teams struggle with alignment. The uncomfortable truth
> is that nobody wants to admit they're confused. And that's okay.

**Wrong fix**
> Teams struggle with alignment. Nobody admits confusion.

*Why wrong: stripped to two flat fragments; sounds like a different machine.*

**Right fix**
> Most teams struggle with alignment because nobody wants to admit they're
> confused.

*One clear sentence that keeps the causal link and cuts the padding.*

---

## Example 3: Business jargon stack

**Before**
> In today's fast-paced landscape, we need to lean into discomfort and navigate
> uncertainty with clarity. This matters because your competition isn't waiting.

**Wrong fix**
> Move faster. Your competition is.

*Why wrong: a punchy quotable. Reads like an ad, which is a slop of its own.*

**Right fix**
> Your competitors are moving quickly, so being comfortable with uncertainty is
> now part of the job.

*Jargon gone, meaning intact, rhythm normal.*

---

## Example 4: Formatting slop

**Before**
> **The problem:** Teams waste time.
> **The cause:** Poor tools.
> **The fix:** Better tools.

**Wrong fix**
> Teams waste time. Poor tools. Better tools.

*Why wrong: kept the fragmentary listicle rhythm, just dropped the bold.*

**Right fix**
> Teams waste time mostly because their tools are poor, so better tools are the
> obvious fix.

*Turned a bolded bullet wall back into a sentence.*

---

## Example 5: Sycophancy + conclusion slop

**Before**
> Great question! There are many factors to consider. Ultimately, at the end of
> the day, it really depends on your specific situation.

**Wrong fix**
> It depends.

*Why wrong: technically de-slopped, but now useless; it threw out the content.*

**Right fix**
> It depends on two things: your budget and your timeline. If either is fixed,
> that decides it.

*Cut the flattery and the empty conclusion; replaced vagueness with specifics.*
