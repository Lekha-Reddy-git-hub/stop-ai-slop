# Sycophancy, hedging, and conclusion slop

Modern AI text (2025 onward) carries tells the original banlists missed. These
come from preference-tuning: the model learned to flatter, hedge, and wrap up.

## Sycophancy (cut)

- "Great question!" / "Great point!" / "That's a fantastic idea."
- "You're absolutely right." / "Excellent observation."
- "I'd be happy to help with that." / "Certainly!" as an opener.
- "What a thoughtful way to put it."

Cut these entirely. They flatter without informing.

## Hedging stacks (thin out)

One hedge is honest. A stack is evasion.
- "It could be argued that perhaps in some cases it might be worth considering..."
  Keep at most one hedge: "In some cases, X."
- "I think maybe it's possible that" → "Possibly X" or just "X."

## Conclusion slop (cut or sharpen)

- "In conclusion," / "To sum up," / "All in all," / "At the end of the day,".
- The paragraph that restates every prior point before ending.
- "Ultimately, it comes down to..." followed by a truism.
- "Only time will tell." / "The future remains to be seen."

A strong ending lands on one specific idea, not a summary of all of them.

## Audience-hailing (cut)

- "Whether you're a beginner or an expert, ..."
- "For anyone looking to ..."
- "In this article, we'll explore ..." (announces instead of doing).

State the content. The reader knows who they are.
