# Channel-specific slop

Slop wears a different costume on each platform. First remove the base tells
(phrases.md, structures.md, formatting.md, sycophancy.md). THEN apply the channel
layer below. Each channel says what slop looks like there and what the rewrite
should optimize for.

Rule that overrides everything: do not replace slop with short-punchy fragments.
That is its own tell. Vary sentence length; write like a specific human, not a
motivational poster.

## X / Twitter posts

Slop looks like:
- Thread-bait openers: "A thread", "Let's dive in", "Here's why", "Buckle up".
- The guru hook-promise-payoff formula every post copies.
- "Nobody is talking about this", "This changed everything for me".
- Engagement-bait closers: "Agree?", "RT if you agree", "Follow for more".

Optimize the rewrite for:
- One idea, stated in the first line, with no hook cliche in front of it.
- A concrete specific (a number, a name, a moment) instead of a general claim.
- Cut the thread-bait. If it genuinely needs a thread, let the content earn it.
- No "follow for more". Let the post stand on its own.

## LinkedIn posts

This is the most recognizable slop on any platform. Slop looks like:
- Bold-unicode fake headers.
- Broetry: one sentence. per line. for false drama.
- "Here's the thing", "Let that sink in", "The result?".
- Emoji section markers before every point.
- Humble-brag setup, then a tidy lesson, then an "Agree?" poll close.
- An IMPORTANT or TL;DR recap block that restates every point.

Optimize the rewrite for:
- Normal paragraphs of two to four sentences.
- One specific claim from lived experience, not a generic summary.
- No bold-unicode, no broetry line breaks, no emoji bullets.
- End on the single strongest idea, not a recap and not a poll.

## Cold email

Slop looks like:
- "I hope this email finds you well", "I wanted to reach out".
- "I came across your profile and was impressed" (fake, could be anyone).
- Three paragraphs before the actual ask.
- "I'd love to pick your brain", "hop on a quick call".

Optimize the rewrite for:
- A first line that could ONLY be sent to this person: specific and real.
- One clear ask, stated early.
- Short. They owe you nothing, so respect the time.
- No pleasantry throat-clearing before the point.

## Warm email

Slop looks like:
- Over-formality with someone you know: "Per my last email", "Kindly find".
- "Just circling back", "Just following up", "Bumping this".
- Corporate hedging with a friend or a close colleague.

Optimize the rewrite for:
- Match the warmth that already exists. Write like you would actually speak
  to them.
- Say what you need plainly; the relationship carries it.
- One light nudge beats three apologetic ones.

## Article / long-form

Slop looks like:
- "In this article, we'll explore" (announcing instead of doing).
- Header-per-paragraph, listicle skeleton, every section title the same shape.
- A conclusion that restates every section.

Optimize the rewrite for:
- Start inside the material, not with a roadmap.
- Headings only for genuine sections, not decoration.
- End on one idea, not a summary of all of them.
