# Advanced patterns catalog (v1.3)

The fuller catalog of AI tells, added to catch up with and go past the current
stop-slop, and to fold in patterns documented by Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing"
project (via the humanizer skill, MIT). Kept tiered: nothing here is a blanket ban.
Cut what is reflexive, keep what the author genuinely does. See false-positives.md
before you cut.

## Structural tells

**Negative listing.** "Not a ticket. Not a checklist. A way of thinking." Building
to a reveal through a stack of negations. Cut the runway, state the thing.

**False agency (name the human).** Inanimate things given human verbs. "The data
tells us," "the market rewards," "the culture shifts," "the decision emerges," "a
complaint becomes a fix." Data sits there until a person reads it. Name the actor,
or use "you" to put the reader in the seat.

**Narrator from a distance.** Floating above the scene: "Nobody designed this,"
"This happens because," "This is why," "People tend to." Put the reader in the
room instead: "You do not sit down one day and decide to..."

**Passive voice (find the actor).** "Mistakes were made," "the decision was
reached," "it is believed that." Passive hides who did it. Name them and move them
to the front. Keep passive only when the actor is genuinely unknown or irrelevant.

**Copula avoidance.** Elaborate substitutes for "is" and "are": "serves as,"
"stands as," "boasts," "features," "represents a." Usually "is" is the honest verb.
"Gallery 825 is the exhibition space," not "serves as."

**Superficial -ing analysis.** Present-participle tails that fake depth:
"..., highlighting the community's connection to the land," "..., reflecting broader
trends," "..., ensuring seamless integration." Cut the tail or make it a real clause
with a real claim.

**False ranges.** "From X to Y" where X and Y are not on a scale: "from the Big
Bang to the cosmic web, from the birth of stars to dark matter." List the actual
items instead.

**Aphorism formulas.** "X is the Y of Z," "the language of trust," "the currency of
attention," "X is not a tool but a mirror." Sounds profound, adds no precision.
Replace with the concrete claim it gestures at.

**Elegant variation.** Cycling synonyms for the same noun to dodge repetition: "the
protagonist... the main character... the central figure... the hero." Just repeat
the word or use a pronoun.

**Diff-anchored writing.** Docs or comments that narrate a change instead of
describing the thing: "This function was added to replace the old approach."
Describe what it is now, unless the document is a changelog.

## Sentence-start tells (frequency-capped, not banned)

**Wh- openers.** Sentences opening with What, When, Where, Which, Who, Why, How as
a setup: "What makes this hard is..." becomes "The constraint is..." Cap these; one
is fine, a habit is a tell.

**"So" and "Look" openers.** Paragraphs starting with "So," sentences starting with
"Look," or standalone "Honestly?" as a fake-candid hook. A person being honest just
says the thing.

## Vocabulary tells (always-cut when reflexive)

**Significance and legacy inflation.** stands as, serves as, is a testament to, a
pivotal/vital/crucial moment, underscores its importance, reflects broader, marking
a shift, evolving landscape, indelible mark, deeply rooted, setting the stage for.

**Promotional language.** boasts, vibrant, rich (figurative), nestled, in the heart
of, breathtaking, must-visit, renowned, stunning, groundbreaking, commitment to.

**AI vocabulary.** delve, tapestry, testament, intricate, interplay, garner,
underscore, showcase, enduring, vibrant, pivotal, foster, align with, leverage,
robust, seamless, elevate.

**Adverbs (tiered, not a blanket kill).** The current stop-slop says "kill ALL
adverbs." deslop does not, because that over-corrects into stilted telegraphic
prose, which is its own tell. Instead:
- Always cut the empty intensifiers that add no information: really, very, just,
  literally, actually, genuinely, honestly, simply, basically, truly, deeply,
  incredibly, extremely, "-ly" hedges like "arguably" and "essentially".
- Replace an adverb propping up a weak verb with a stronger verb: "ran quickly"
  becomes "sprinted"; "said angrily" becomes "snapped"; "very big" becomes "huge".
- KEEP adverbs that carry real meaning: legally, remotely, quarterly, clinically,
  publicly, privately, and any -ly word that changes what the sentence claims.
  "She is legally liable" cannot lose "legally".

**Vague attribution and weasel words.** "Experts believe," "Industry reports,"
"Observers have noted," "Some critics argue," "studies show" with no named source.
Name the source or cut the claim.

**Vague declaratives.** "The reasons are structural," "the implications are
significant," "the stakes are high," "this is the deepest problem." Announces
importance without naming the specific thing. Cut it or show the specific thing.

**Persuasive authority tropes.** "The real question is," "at its core," "in
reality," "what really matters," "fundamentally," "the deeper issue," "the heart of
the matter." Ceremony in front of an ordinary point.

## Formatting tells (see also formatting.md)

Curly quotes where straight quotes belong (auto-curling is not itself a tell, but
paired with others it is). Title Case In Headings (use sentence case). Uniform
hyphenation of word pairs in predicate position ("the report is high-quality" should
be "high quality"; keep the hyphen only when attributive: "a high-quality report").
